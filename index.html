What is deliverance

Deliverance is Jesus setting people free from what no effort of their own can break.

Deliverance is the ministry of freeing a person from spiritual bondage, oppression, torment, addiction, fear, and patterns that hold them captive against their own will. It is not therapy and it is not willpower. It is the authority of Jesus Christ, applied in love and prayer, to break chains that the natural world cannot explain.

Throughout the Gospels, Jesus did this constantly and He gave the same authority to those who follow Him. What He did then, He still does now. The work is gentle, confidential, and rooted entirely in scripture.

Signs to look for

You may be facing something spiritual not just something natural.

Many struggles have natural causes and we never assume otherwise. But when these patterns are persistent, unexplainable, or stronger than your own will to change, they can point to a need for deliverance.

Sickness & infirmity Depression Addiction Money problems Anger problems Can't stop swearing Suicidal thoughts Feeling cursed Seeing things no one else sees Constant nightmares Hearing voices that tear you down Memory loss & forgetfulness Sinusitis problems Constant migraines or headaches Eyesight problems Arthritis Always stressing about things
Spiritual & moral

Struggles of the will

  • Habitual addictions. The urge overpowers your genuine desire to stop substances, pornography, or self-harm.
  • Spiritual resistance. Sudden sleepiness, agitation, or distraction when you try to pray, worship, or read scripture.
  • Heavy oppression. Repeated nightmares of demonic figures or death, or a physical weight on your body or in your home.
Mental & emotional

Torment within

  • Guilt & shame. Persistent torment from past sins even after you have repented and been forgiven.
  • Fear & paranoia. Severe, unexplainable paranoia or intrusive thoughts of violence and suicide you cannot shake.
  • Internal division. Feeling like two people one longing for God, one constantly sabotaging your life and faith.
Relational & life

Patterns that follow you

  • Constant chaos. Conflict, broken relationships, and sudden failures follow you with no clear explanation.
  • Generational cycles. Repeating trends in your family divorces, recurring illness, or poverty.

Recognise yourself here? You are not weak and you are not alone. Reaching out is the first step into the light and it costs you nothing.
